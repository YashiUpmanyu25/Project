Objectives

  • Primary Objective: To reduce food waste by redistributing surplus food from weddings to feed the hungry.
  • Secondary Objectives: To raise awareness about food insecurity, promote community engagement, and foster a culture of sharing and sustainability.

Key Skateholders

Donors:
Wedding couples, event planners, caterers, and venue owners who are willing to donate surplus food.
Beneficiaries:
Local shelters, food banks, and community centers that will receive and distribute the donated food.
Partners:
Nonprofit organizations, local government agencies, and volunteers who will assist in the collection and distribution process.
Volunteers:
Individuals and groups who will help with logistics, including collection, transportation, and distribution of food.

Process and Implementation

  1. Partnership Development: Collaborate with wedding planners, venues, and caterers to secure commitments for food donations.
  2. Collection: Set up a streamlined process for collecting surplus food at the end of wedding events. This will include providing containers and transportation for the food.
  3. Distribution: Partner with local shelters and food banks to ensure the collected food is promptly delivered and safely distributed to those in need.
  4. Awareness Campaign: Conduct campaigns to raise awareness about food wastage and the benefits of food donations. This will involve social media outreach, flyers, and community events.
  5. Monitoring and Evaluation: Implement a system to track the amount of food collected, distributed, and the number of beneficiaries. Regularly evaluate the effectiveness of the project and make necessary adjustments.
